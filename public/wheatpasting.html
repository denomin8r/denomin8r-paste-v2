Wheatpasting

Wheatpasting is the art of applying posters to public surfaces using a paste made from wheat flour and water. It's a traditional method of street art that has been used for decades to share messages, art, and information in public spaces.

What You'll Need

  • Wheat paste: Mix 1 part wheat flour with 2 parts water, cook until thickened
  • Brushes: Large paint brushes or wallpaper brushes work best
  • Posters: Your artwork printed on paper (newsprint or similar)
  • Bucket: To carry your paste
  • Scraper: To remove old posters if needed

Basic Technique

  1. Prepare your wheat paste and let it cool
  2. Clean the surface you want to paste on
  3. Apply paste to the surface first
  4. Place your poster and smooth it out
  5. Apply more paste over the top
  6. Use a brush to remove air bubbles

Tips & Safety

  • Always check local laws and regulations
  • Respect private property
  • Use biodegradable paste when possible
  • Work quickly - wheat paste dries fast
  • Consider the weather - avoid rain

Wheat Paste Recipe

Ingredients: - 1 cup wheat flour - 2 cups water - 1/4 cup sugar (optional, helps with adhesion) Instructions: 1. Mix flour and water in a pot 2. Heat on medium, stirring constantly 3. Cook until mixture thickens (about 5-10 minutes) 4. Let cool before using 5. Store in refrigerator for up to 1 week
