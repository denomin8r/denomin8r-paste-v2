Wheatpasting

Wheatpasting is the art of applying posters to public surfaces using a paste made from wheat flour and water. This page provides resources for doing wheatpasting on your own.

What You'll Need

  • Wheat paste: Homemade or store-bought adhesive
  • Brushes: Large paint brushes or wallpaper brushes
  • Posters: Your artwork printed on appropriate paper (newsprint, kraft paper, or similar thin, absorbent paper works best)
  • Bucket: To carry your paste
  • Scraper: To remove old posters if needed
  • Drop cloth: To protect the ground while working

Make Your Own Wheatpaste

Basic Wheatpaste Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup wheat flour (all-purpose or bread flour)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup sugar (optional, helps with adhesion)
  • 1/4 cup white glue (optional, for extra strength)

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour and water in a pot until smooth
  2. Heat on medium heat, stirring constantly
  3. Cook until mixture thickens to a paste consistency (about 5-10 minutes)
  4. Remove from heat and let cool completely
  5. Store in refrigerator for up to 1 week

Basic Technique

  1. Prepare: Make sure your wheatpaste is cool and at the right consistency
  2. Clean: Remove loose debris from the surface you want to paste on
  3. Apply base paste: Use a brush to apply paste directly to the surface
  4. Position poster: Carefully place your poster and smooth it out from the center
  5. Cover with paste: Apply more paste over the top of the poster
  6. Remove bubbles: Use a brush to smooth out any air bubbles or wrinkles
  7. Let dry: Allow the paste to dry completely (usually 15-30 minutes)

Tips and Safety

  • Legal considerations: Always check local laws and regulations regarding wheatpasting
  • Property respect: Avoid private property and respect community spaces
  • Weather: Avoid pasting in rain or high humidity - wheatpaste needs to dry
  • Surface selection: Choose clean, smooth surfaces for best adhesion
  • Work quickly: Wheatpaste dries fast, so have everything ready before starting
  • Clean up: Bring a rag to clean up any spills or drips
  • Safety: Work in well-lit areas and be aware of your surroundings
